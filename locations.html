The Tourism of the Torii

Throughout the year, millions fly into Japan to explore the rich and beautiful cities the country has to offer. It just so happens that within these beautiful cities lies famous torii gates which act as huge tourist hotspots with almost everybody wanting to snap a click of their camera on the gaze of the torii.


The years pass, yet the millions of tourists always return for the glimpse of the ethereal torii's standing still in serentity yet leaving such a powerful emotion for the Japanese and followers of Shintoism.


Lets explore the most famous torii gates in Japan together!

Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kyoto

Fushimi Inari Shrine is one of, if not the most famous shinto shrine in all of Japan, it is known for it's trail of plentiful torii gates wrapped around a beautiful vermillion color. The shrine is dedicated to Inari (the shinto god of rice)


Foxes are said to be messengers of the god Inari therefore around the shrine are many statues paying homage to the fox. It is also quite symbolic that the colors of the torii in Fushimi Inari Shrine are all vermillion with black engravings akin to the color of a foxes pelt.

The Senbon Torii Trail

The Senbon Torii Trail at Fushimi Inari Shrine leading to the shrine and the peak of Mount Inari is a trail of a thousand... yes a thousand torii gates although, across the entire mountain/shrine there are believed to be over 10,000 torii gates in all sizes near the shrines. These torii gates are among the most visited in all of Japan for it's sheer beauty and sense of spirtituality from it's colors and it's location enveloped in peace and nature.

Meiji Shrine, Tokyo

Tokyo is home to one of the most towering and jaw-dropping torii gates at the Meiji Jingu. The torii that is located at the entrance of the Meiji Shrine was constructed to honor the late Emperor Meiji. Meiji Shrine annualy is a popular location for tourists and countryfolk during the new year amounting to 3 million visitors per year.


The torii is surrounded by a forest and has grown to adapt to it's surroundings by taking on a mossy green color for the "kasagi" (top piece of the torii) making for a breathtaking image for tourists around the world.

An interesting fact about this torii is that it isn't the original!. The original torii gate that marked the entrance to Meiji Shrine in 1920 was destroyed by lightning in 1966 so a new gate was built in 1975 which is this ever so famous torii standing tall at 12 meters of height and over 17 meters in width making it the largest wooden torii on the planet!


As people move and the forest shakes with the wind, the torii remains still taking in the beauty of nature...

Miyajima, Hiroshima Bay

Miyajima is a very old and traditional shrine in Japan, with the name directly translating to "shrine island". It's towering torii gate lies as the main tourist attraction at the shrine.


The beautiful gate stands still against the crashing and ever moving tides in Hiroshima Bay. The torii gate during the high tide almost looks as if it's floating making for a gorgeous memory which welcomes millions of tourists to return to this beautiful spot. The "shrine island" Miyajima is known across Japan for it's long history and strong connection as a unique sight of shintoism.
